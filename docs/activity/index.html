3.1 | Hello World

Hello World is a daily show that airs on Microsoft Learn TV. It launched Feb 22 and I began co-hosting the Thursday edition on Mar 11.

On Mar 25 I debuted the A-B-See segment, the first to engage guests in interactive challenges by presenting them with visual puzzles (each featuring a concept, service, or person) that tested their tech IQ.

I contributed to Season 1 by co-hosting 14 episodes (Thursdays) with 36 A-B-See puzzles played.

3.2 Azure Functions

The AzureFunctions: OpenAPI and Power Apps event gave a 2-hour introduction to fusion development with focus on using OpenAPI, Azure Functions and Power Apps to create a low-code friendly app development workflow.

Learn more about the event here and watch session replays here. You can complete the learning path here. My contributions included 6 intersitials, 1 visual guide, and multiple blog posts.

  • Watch the livestream recording to spot the puzzles
  • Visit the A-B-See site to explore the clues
  • Read the blog post to explore the illustrated guide.
  • Visit the dev.to blog series for more updates

4.1 | Topics I Talk About

I like to talk about visual storytelling at different levels. If you’d like me to speak at your community, conference, or internal, event - do reach out! My DMs are open..

Level Topic Description
100 Visual Storytelling Introduction, Motivation, Examples
200 Learn to Sketch Toolkits, Techniques, Roadmap
300 Sketch
to Learn		 Visual Guides, Sketch-to-Slide, Accessibility
300 Sketch
to Engage		 Interstitials, Visual Games, Visual Glossary

My roadmap for visual storytelling learner journeys:

  1. Sketchnoting - toolkits & techniques
  2. Anthropomorphism - characters & emotion
  3. Metaphors - connections & transfer
  4. Medium - guides, curriculum, interstitials
  5. Practice - synthesis, speed, storytelling
